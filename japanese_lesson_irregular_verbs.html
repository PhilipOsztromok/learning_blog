⚡ Irregular Verbs — ふきそくどうし（不規則動詞）

The great news: Japanese has only two truly irregular verbs — する (to do) and くる (to come). The rest of this lesson shows how する combines with nouns to create hundreds of useful compound verbs.

Why just two? Unlike English (go/went, be/was/been…) or European languages with dozens of irregular verbs, Japanese keeps it remarkably tidy. する and くる（来る） are the only verbs that don't follow either the godan or ichidan rules — their stems change completely depending on the form. Memorise these two well, because they are among the most frequently used verbs in the entire language.

The する superpower — unlocking hundreds of verbs: Once you know how する conjugates, every compound する verb comes for free. Any noun — Japanese (べんきょう, りょこう) or borrowed from English (チェックイン, ダウンロード) — can become a verb by simply adding する. So チェックインします = "I check in" and ダウンロードします = "I download". You will encounter dozens of these in daily life in Japan. As for くる — watch the nai form carefully: it is こない, not くない. That change from く → こ trips up almost everyone at first.
